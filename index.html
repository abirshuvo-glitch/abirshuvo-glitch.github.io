01 — About

Who I am and how I think about security

Security + startups. I care about building systems people actually use, not just theory — from shopkeeper apps in Bangladesh to clean incident workflows in the SOC.

I’m a second-year Cybersecurity student with a background in Python, SQL, HTML/CSS, networking, and security fundamentals (OWASP, MFA, zero-trust thinking). I like taking messy, ambiguous situations and turning them into clear, documented workflows that a stressed team can follow at 2 AM.

I’m also the founder of DokanBondhu, a smart ledger & sales platform for Bangladeshi grocery shopkeepers. That project forces me to think about real users, real constraints, and real risk: bad devices, bad connectivity, shared phones, and a lot of human factors.

Right now I’m pushing deeper into OWASP Top 10, Linux hardening, and cloud basics, while building work that looks like what a junior SOC analyst or security engineer actually does.
